20 – 99

💡 Note

German builds compound numbers by saying the unit FIRST, then 'und', then the ten.
21 = einundzwanzig (one-and-twenty) — opposite to English!
The -zig suffix sounds like [-tsish].

✅ Tip

Pattern for -zig numbers: unit + und + ten
e.g. 63 = dreiundsechzig · 78 = achtundsiebzig
Note: dreißig (30) is spelled with -ßig, not -zig — it's the only exception!

Hundreds (100 – 900)

💡 Note

Hundreds are formed by placing the digit before hundert.
100 can be einhundert or just hundert. No space is written between parts.
