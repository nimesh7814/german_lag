The German Alphabet

💡 Note

German has 26 standard letters plus 4 special characters: Ä/ä, Ö/ö, Ü/ü (Umlauts) and ß (Eszett).
ALL nouns are capitalized in German, regardless of position in the sentence!

✅ Tip

Spelling out a name in German: Germans use city names as reference.
e.g. "H wie Hamburg, E wie Emil, R wie Richard" = H-E-R
Use: A-Anton, B-Berta, C-Cäsar, D-Dora, E-Emil, F-Friedrich, G-Gustav, H-Heinrich, I-Ida, J-Julius, K-Kaufmann, L-Ludwig, M-Martha, N-Nordpol, O-Otto, P-Paula, Q-Quelle, R-Richard, S-Samuel, T-Theodor, U-Ulrich, V-Viktor, W-Wilhelm, X-Xanthippe, Y-Ypsilon, Z-Zacharias

✅ Tip

No umlaut on your keyboard? Write: ä = ae, ö = oe, ü = ue, ß = ss.
Example: Müller = Mueller, Straße = Strasse.
